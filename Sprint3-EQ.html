Sprint 3 Analogy

What is emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to be in tune with oneself and also being able to read situations from verbal and non verbal ques, such as body language / behaviour and whether people are comfortable or not.

Emotional Intelligence for myself is knowing what I am doing, and controlling myself. One of my favourite sayings is that ”I like to eat desert at the end”, For instance like to reverse the car in, so when I drive out I am driving forward and appreciate the effort gone in before. I like to do the hard work first, and enjoy the fruits of my effort later

Emotional Intelligence also encompasses identifying my own emotions and what affect they have on me and how I portray. How do I react in good and not so good situations, am I calm for instance

People who have good Emotional Intelligence skills, are those that get on well with people

I am going through a challenging time, being made redundant at the end of the year, and I know that this is stressing me, and I feel a need to get another job, but I need to make sure I go for the job that I want to do, and not any job, just to tick the “I have a job” box

Differences with Intellectual ability

Intellectual ability is more about being smart, and good at learning and thinking about doing tasks

Why is it Important

Important to understand both concepts, as we need to know why from an individuals perspective how we are achieving something and are we combing both to good at our job
